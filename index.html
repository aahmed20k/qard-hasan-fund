The problem we solve

Halal financing shouldn't be a competition

Interest-free funding in the U.S. flows through a handful of national providers with finite capital — forcing applicants to compete on metrics that quietly shut out lower- and middle-tier families who are perfectly trustworthy.

Scarce & centralized

One or two organizations, limited funds. Demand vastly outstrips what any single national pool can serve.

Metric-gated

Applicants are ranked against one another. Middle-tier and student applicants lose out despite genuine need.

Impersonal

Distance means no relationship — and no way to lend on character, references, or local trust.

How it works

One fund, two carefully separated pockets

Waqf, zakat and qard hasan follow different rules — so we never mix them. Every donation is routed at the moment you give.

Pocket A · General / Waqf

The lending engine

  • A permanent endowment whose income — plus general sadaqah and repaid principal — funds the loans.
  • Pays out interest-free qard hasan that recycles as it's repaid.
  • 501(c)(3) tax-deductible for donors.
Pocket B · Zakat

The grant & forgiveness arm

  • Given only as outright grants to eligible recipients — transferred into their ownership.
  • Forgives the balance for borrowers who become unable to repay and qualify.
  • Never lent. Never spent on operations. Kept in a separate ledger.
